# Swarnix — Inbound Growth Strategy

*An organic-first playbook for getting the right Indian jewellers to find Swarnix on their own.*
Company: Nelishka AI Solutions (nelishkaai.in) · Product: Swarnix (swarnixai.in)

---

## The thesis (read this first)

Every channel here exists to put **one real jeweller's result in front of the next jeweller, in the places they already trust.** You are not selling software to people who distrust salespeople — you are making "going digital" look like the normal thing a successful peer two shops down already does.

That's the whole machine. SEO, social, associations, shows — each one is just a different surface for the same proof, and each one ends in the same place: a WhatsApp message to your demo bot, where the buyer experiences the product on the platform they live on.

## 1. GOOGLE (Search + SEO)

### The play
Own the tiny set of searches only a jeweller who *already wants* to modernise would type. Don't chase consumer try-on traffic. The buyer queries in India are low-volume but almost uncontested — you can rank fast, and the intent is as warm as it gets.

### The why
A tech-resistant owner won't Google "AI chatbot." But the younger son running the counter, or the owner who has already decided he's tired of missing after-hours enquiries, *will* type "whatsapp chatbot for jewellery shop." That person is your cheapest, warmest lead. There aren't many of them per month — but there's barely any competition for them either, so you don't need a budget to win, you need the page to exist and be the clearest answer.

### Step by step

**A. Google Business Profile (free, do it Week 1)**
Set Nelishka up as a **service-area business**, not a storefront. You qualify because you meet jewellers in person across Mumbai/Maharashtra — a purely online company with zero in-person contact does *not* qualify, so this matters. Practically:
- Leave the street address hidden. Set the **service area** (Mumbai, Thane, Navi Mumbai, Pune, then the rest of Maharashtra).
- Primary category: **Software company** (pick the closest available; add a second category if the picker offers a good fit).
- Add the WhatsApp number, the demo link, your hours, the brand logo (bronze/charcoal "S"), and 8–10 photos (product screens, the brochure, you at work).
- Post weekly using GBP Posts — short proof clips or tips. It keeps the profile "active," which Google rewards.
- **Reviews are the trust lever.** Conservative buyers check them. As you land jewellers, ask each one (after they've seen a result) to leave a one-line review. Five honest reviews from named local shops beats any ad.

> This is the same kind of profile you'd see for a small local service business — a clean card on Search and Maps with reviews, photos, and a contact button. That credibility signal is worth more to a suspicious buyer than your website is.

**B. On-page SEO — swarnixai.in landing page**
- **Title tag** (~55–60 chars): `Swarnix — WhatsApp AI Sales Agent for Jewellers`
- **Meta description** (~150 chars): `An AI sales agent on your shop's WhatsApp — answers customers in Hindi, Marathi & Gujarati, shows try-ons, never sleeps. See a live demo.`
- **One H1**, clear: *"The 24/7 salesperson that lives on your shop's WhatsApp."*
- **H2s built around proof + benefit, not features:** "Replies to customers the moment you can't," "Customers try on jewellery from their sofa," "Works in the language your customer speaks," "Made for jewellery shops, not generic bots."
- **One CTA, repeated:** *"See a 2-minute live demo on WhatsApp"* → opens a WhatsApp chat with your demo bot. This is your single biggest advantage — the buyer experiences the product instantly, on home turf, instead of filling a form.
- **Mobile-first and fast.** Your audience opens this on a phone. Compress images, keep it light.

**C. On-page SEO — nelishkaai.in**
Position this as the company (the parent), linking down to Swarnix. Add an About page with your face and the "why I built this" story (founder credibility carries weight in this trade), and a Proof/Case-study page (even one real story once you have it). Cross-link both sites.

**D. Schema markup (free, high payoff)**
Add JSON-LD to the Swarnix landing page:
- `Organization` (Nelishka) + `SoftwareApplication` (Swarnix, with `applicationCategory: BusinessApplication`)
- `FAQPage` — answer the real buyer questions ("Does it work in Marathi?", "Do I need an app?", "Will it copy my designs?"). FAQ schema can win extra space on the results page.
- `BreadcrumbList` for any sub-pages.

**E. Internal linking**
Landing page → each how-to page → back to a demo CTA. Each regional-language page links to its English twin and vice versa. The Proof page links out to every comparison and how-to page. Keep the demo CTA reachable from every page in one click.

- Location: Maharashtra + Surat + Jaipur (your nearest jewellery clusters).
- Send clicks to the demo-CTA landing page, count WhatsApp demo opens as the conversion.
- Kill any keyword where cost-per-demo exceeds what a demo is worth to you. If the whole test produces nothing, you've learned the channel is too thin — for ₹6,000 that's a cheap lesson.

## 2. FACEBOOK

### The play
Facebook is a **credibility surface**, not a growth engine — plus deep, value-first presence in the jeweller groups where owners already gather. No mass-tagging. No link-dumping. Earned attention only.

### The why
A suspicious jeweller checks whether you're real. An active page full of peer results and a founder's face tells him you're a business, not a fly-by-night. And jewellery owners — especially the 35–55 crowd — sit in regional Facebook groups and trade WhatsApp groups all day. That's where peer behaviour is visible. You get pulled in by being the useful "WhatsApp/AI person" in those rooms, never by pitching.

### Step by step
1. **Page setup (Week 1):** Complete every field. Link website + WhatsApp, set the message button to open WhatsApp, add the demo link, the brand visuals, and a Services section. Pin a 60–90s proof video to the top. Don't put price on the page.
2. **Content pillars** (proof over feature-listing):
   - **Peer wins / before–after** — a jeweller's enquiry count, a try-on that closed a sale (real + opted-in only).
   - **Demos** — a 20–40s screen recording of the bot doing *one* thing (answering at 11pm, showing a try-on, quoting today's gold rate).
   - **Owner education** — "How to handle the customer who says 'just send rate on WhatsApp'."
   - **Trust / who you are** — short founder notes, why you built this.
   - **Industry moments** — festival readiness, gold-rate days, an IIJS recap.
3. **Cadence:** 3–4 posts a week. Sustainable beats heroic. A dead page that posted daily for two weeks then went silent reads worse than a steady one.
4. **Groups strategy:**
   - **Find them:** search "jewellers", "jewellery business", "swarnkar", "sarafa", "gold jewellery sellers", and `[city] + jewellers`. Look for the GJC/association-linked groups. Join 5–10 active ones.
   - **The 90/10 rule:** 90% give, 10% mention. Answer questions, drop a genuinely useful tip, share a free resource (e.g. "festival WhatsApp reply templates"). Mention Swarnix only when it directly answers what someone asked.
   - **Become known before you sell.** When members start tagging *you* on "how do I automate my WhatsApp" threads, the pull has started.

### Compliance — do not skip
- **No mass-tagging or unsolicited tagging** of jewellery owners. It violates platform policy, reads as spam to a tight community where reputation travels fast, and risks your account getting flagged or banned.
- **No cold DMs to strangers**, no posting your link in every thread.
- **Earned tagging only:** a happy customer tags you, or you tag a customer who *opted in* to be featured.

### Ready-to-use — Facebook posts
**Post A — peer win**
> A jeweller in [city] told me last week: "I used to lose customers who messaged after I shut the shop. Now they get an answer at 11pm — and three of them walked in the next morning."
> That's the whole point. Your shop's WhatsApp shouldn't go quiet when you do.
> [link: see a 2-min demo on WhatsApp]

**Post B — demo**
> Watch this. A customer sends a selfie. Seconds later she sees the necklace on herself — no app, no AR setup, all inside WhatsApp.
> This is running on a real jeweller's number right now.
> Want to try it on yours? Message us → [demo link]

**Post C — owner education (no pitch)**
> If your customer's first message is "rate kya hai?", you've got about 90 seconds before she messages the next shop too.
> Three things that keep her with you: a fast reply, today's exact rate, and a photo of something close to what she wants. Speed wins this customer, not price.

### Ready-to-use — Facebook group opener
*(Use only after you've engaged genuinely with the group/person first — a reply on their post, a useful comment. This is a soft DM, not a blast.)*
> Hi [name] — saw your question in [group] about handling WhatsApp enquiries after hours. I work with jewellers on exactly this. Happy to show you what's possible in a 2-minute demo, no pressure — or just tell me your setup and I'll point you in the right direction either way.

## 3. INSTAGRAM

### The play
Instagram is your **proof-reel engine aimed at owners** (not end-consumers), geo + hashtag targeted to Indian jewellers, plus *compliant* DM conversations with owners who show buying signals. The single highest-value asset: one respected jeweller's win, on camera.

### The why
The younger family decision-maker and the design-forward owner live on Instagram. Reels travel. A 20-second clip of a customer doing a WhatsApp try-on, or a peer owner saying "I get enquiries at 11pm now," outperforms any feature list — because it's a peer, doing the thing, getting a result.

### Step by step
1. **Profile (Week 1):** Business account. Bio: *"WhatsApp AI sales agent for jewellers · Mumbai · 2-min demo ↓"* with the demo/link tool in the bio. Highlights: **Demo · Try-On · Peer Wins · FAQ.** Brand visuals throughout.
2. **Content pillars:** same five as Facebook, but **Reels-first**, because that's where the reach is.
3. **Reels cadence:** 1 strong "hero" reel a week + 2–3 lighter posts. The hooks must stop an *owner's* scroll, not a shopper's:
   - "If your shop's WhatsApp goes silent after 9pm, you're losing sales."
   - "Your competitor's customer is trying on jewellery from her sofa right now."
   - "I gave a jeweller a salesperson who never takes a day off. Here's what happened."
   - "Stop typing the gold rate 40 times a day."
4. **Hashtags + geo:** use **5–10 relevant tags, not 30**. Mix trade tags (`#jewellerybusiness #jewelleryretail #indianjeweller #goldjewellery #jewelleryshop #sarafa`) with city/regional tags (`#mumbaijewellers #punejewellers` — note many city tags skew consumer). Geo-tag the shop's city, and the venue during a trade show. **Reality check:** hashtag reach is far weaker than it was 2–3 years ago — the algorithm now rewards watch-time and saves more than tags. Treat hashtags as a small boost, not the strategy.
5. **Compliant DM outreach** — only to owners showing a **buying signal** (commented, asked a question, follows you, liked several posts, or whose content you genuinely engaged with first):
   - Lead with something specific about *their* shop, not a pitch.
   - Offer value (a demo, or a useful tip), no link-dump, no copy-paste blast.
   - **What NOT to do:** cold mass DMs, automated DM tools, "Hi dear, interested??" spam, messaging people who never engaged. Instagram throttles and flags accounts that blast DMs — and it burns your reputation in a small trade.
6. **Get one peer on camera:** find your first willing customer (or a friendly early adopter). Film 30–60s: them at the counter, one line on the result, one screen-grab of the bot working. Give them something for it — a free month, a co-branded reel, a feature. **This one asset triggers more peer pull than everything else combined.**

### Ready-to-use — Instagram captions + Reel hooks
**Reel 1**
- Hook (on-screen, first 2 sec): *"Your shop closes. Your WhatsApp shouldn't."*
- Caption: A jeweller in [city] stopped losing after-hours customers the day his WhatsApp started replying without him. No app. No staff. Just an AI agent that knows his stock and speaks his customer's language. Want it on your number? Demo link in bio.

**Reel 2**
- Hook: *"She tried on this necklace without leaving her sofa."*
- Caption: Customer sends a selfie → sees the piece on herself → walks in to buy. All inside WhatsApp, no AR. This is live on a real jeweller's number. DM "DEMO" and I'll show you on yours.

**Reel 3**
- Hook: *"Stop typing today's gold rate 40 times a day."*
- Caption: Every "rate kya hai?" answered instantly, with today's exact rate and a photo of something close to what she wants. You handle the sale; the agent handles the 40 messages before it. Demo in bio.

### Ready-to-use — Instagram DM opener
> Hey [name] — your [specific piece / latest post] caught my eye, that [detail] is gorgeous. Quick one: do you handle all your shop's WhatsApp enquiries yourself? I build a WhatsApp AI agent for jewellers that covers the after-hours ones and even does selfie try-ons. Happy to show you a 2-min demo on your own number if you're curious — no pitch, just show you what's possible.

## 4. OTHER CHANNELS *(this is where most of the inbound edge lives)*

### A. Trade bodies, associations & forums
This is the trust shortcut. A conservative owner who ignores a stranger will listen to his own association.

- **GJC — All India Gem & Jewellery Domestic Council** (Mumbai HQ, the national domestic federation representing 6 lakh+ gem & jewellery businesses, with affiliated state associations and a **NextGen** wing for younger jewellers). The NextGen circle is your sweet spot — that's the digital-forward segment. Become a member or affiliate; offer to run a free "WhatsApp/AI for your shop" session at a regional meet; get featured by a respected member rather than pitching cold. GJC also runs the **India Gem & Jewellery Show (GJS)** each year.
- **GJEPC — Gem & Jewellery Export Promotion Council** runs the **IIJS** shows and **Innov8-style talks** — past editions have literally included an "AI in jewellery" session. Apply to speak or demo there; the room is full of retailers and owners.
- **IBJA — India Bullion & Jewellers Association** (you already pull IBJA rates into the product). A relationship here is credibility plus a natural tie-in to your live gold-rate feature.
- **Regional / state associations** — Tamil Nadu Jewellers Federation, Karnataka Jewellers Federation, UP Sarafa Association, Rajkot Gems & Jewellery Association, Bhartiya Swarnkar Sangh (Jaipur), Odisha Jewellers Association, and your local **Mumbai/Thane/Maharashtra sarafa associations** [verify exact names + membership process]. These run the local meets where conservative owners actually show up and trust the man next to them.
- **How to get visibility, in order of effort-to-payoff:** join → get a newsletter/WhatsApp-group mention → speak at a meet → sponsor a small prize at an event → get one respected member to vouch (earned, never spam).

### B. Trade shows & exhibitions (cheap, as a solo founder)
The next big Mumbai show after mid-2026 is **IIJS Premiere (typically August, Mumbai)** [verify exact 2026 dates on gjepc.org], then **GJS (~April)** and **IIJS Signature (~January)** in 2027. A full booth is expensive — so don't take one for your first show. Instead:
- **Get on a panel / Innov8-style talk** — far more inbound pull than a stall, and often free or cheap.
- **Share a small kiosk or partner's stand** — co-demo from a complementary vendor's booth (POS/ERP, packaging, jewellery photography). [confirm partner]
- **Walk it as a visitor + run show-timed content:** post a reel "I'm at IIJS — come see a live WhatsApp try-on," geo-tag the venue. That pulls curious owners *to you*.
- **Your booth is a phone.** A single device running a live WhatsApp demo on your number, your v12 brochure on the table, and a QR that opens the demo bot. The demo *is* the booth — you don't need fixtures.

### C. WhatsApp communities & broadcast (your audience lives here)
- **Join jeweller WhatsApp groups** through associations, trade contacts and referrals. Same 90/10 value rule as Facebook groups.
- **Create a WhatsApp Channel** (the public one-to-many broadcast surface). Post short proof clips and tips; jewellers can follow without giving you their number. **Note:** this is the compliant broadcast tool now — old-style broadcast *lists* only deliver to people who've saved your number, so they don't work for reaching strangers. Use a Channel.
- **The demo bot is your universal CTA** across *every* channel above. "Message us on WhatsApp" → instant product experience, on the buyer's own turf. This is your single biggest unfair advantage; make it the end of every funnel.

### D. YouTube & short-form (the long-tail engine)
Indian jewellers watch a lot of YouTube — the gold-rate channels pull lakhs of subscribers. How-to videos rank and earn for years.
- Post 60–90s **Shorts** (repurpose your reels) + a few longer how-tos: "How to set up WhatsApp selling for your jewellery shop," "Virtual try-on demo," "How I built a 24/7 sales agent for a jeweller."
- Write titles for search intent, pin the demo link in the description and first comment.

### E. LinkedIn (B2B credibility + the digital-forward owners)
- Post as the **face of Nelishka** — build-in-public notes, lessons, demos, peer wins. This reaches the more digital owners, GJC NextGen members, potential partners, and press.
- Cadence: 2–3 posts/week, plus genuine comments on jewellery-industry posts. Connect with retail leaders, NextGen jewellers, and ERP/POS partners.

### F. Free directories & SaaS listings
- **India directories:** Google Business Profile (above), **IndiaMART** (jewellers actually search it for suppliers/software), **JustDial**, **Sulekha** [verify category fit]. These give you branded-search coverage and a few backlinks.
- **SaaS listing sites:** G2, Capterra, GetApp, SaaSHub, Product Hunt (a launch), Crozdesk, AlternativeTo [verify which accept free listings in your category]. Lower lead volume for a niche this specific, but free backlinks and a credibility check for buyers who research before they message.
- **Niche:** any jewellery-industry / association vendor directory you can get listed in.

### G. The referral / peer-proof loop — **your #1 mechanism**
Given the audience psychology, this matters more than any other tactic on this page. Each early jeweller becomes the trust, the proof, and the peer behaviour that pulls in the next. Design it on purpose:
- **At onboarding, get a feature opt-in.** Once they see a result, you turn it into a 30–60s reel, a one-line quote, a logo on the Proof page, and a WhatsApp Channel post.
- **Referral incentive that fits this trade:** a fee waiver or credit beats swag. e.g. one free month, or ₹X off the ₹21,000 onboarding, for each jeweller they refer who signs up [decide exact amount]. Money/fee-relief talks; mugs don't.
- **Cluster strategy.** Jewellery is geographically dense — Zaveri Bazaar in Mumbai, the equivalent bazaar in every city. Land **one respected shop in a cluster**, make their win *loud locally*, and let proximity and peer envy do the selling. The owner moves when the shop two doors down is already doing it.
- **A forwardable WhatsApp proof message.** A short image + one line + demo link that a happy owner can forward into their jeweller WhatsApp groups in one tap. Forwarding is the native sharing behaviour here — engineer for it.

### H. Regional-language content (a reach multiplier, not a translation chore)
- **Hindi, Marathi, Gujarati first** (your Mumbai/Maharashtra base + the Surat/Gujarat hubs). Add Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali as you expand into those clusters.
- Regional **reels, landing pages, and Channel posts.** Competitors publish in English only — regional content reaches the *elder* decision-maker and the smaller-town owner, and ranks for regional searches with almost no competition. This is one of the cheapest edges you have.

## Note for the founder

**Assumptions I made**
- swarnixai.in and nelishkaai.in are live and yours (from your brief). I built every CTA around a **WhatsApp demo bot** because you already run a trial number — if that demo isn't ready to hand to a stranger, that's the first thing to fix, because it's the spine of this whole plan.
- I kept **price out of all public copy on purpose.** Your audience reads a cheap price as suspicious, and the ₹21,000 onboarding is your real friction point — so you sell trust, proof, and a free demo, and surface numbers only in conversation. [Confirm current pricing before you publish anything with figures in it.]

**Things in the brief I'd push back on**
- **"virtual try on jewellery online"** and **"jewellery design AI tool"** are mostly *consumer* searches, not jeweller searches. Ranking for them brings shoppers, not shop owners. Chase the *"...for jewellers / software / business"* variants instead, or you'll get traffic that never converts to B2B.
- **Google Business Profile:** a pure online SaaS with zero in-person contact technically does **not** qualify. You qualify only because you meet jewellers in person in Maharashtra — so set it up as a **service-area business**, hide the address, set the service area, and don't fake a storefront. Faking it gets the profile suspended.
- **Trade-show booth:** don't pay for a full stand at your first IIJS. A panel slot, a partner's booth, or visitor + show-timed content gets you more inbound for far less money.
- **Hashtags and broadcast lists:** Instagram hashtag reach is much weaker than it used to be (watch-time and saves matter more now), and WhatsApp broadcast *lists* only reach people who've saved your number. Use a **WhatsApp Channel** for real broadcast, and treat hashtags as a minor boost.
- **Comparison page naming a competitor:** only build "Swarnix vs [X]" if you can be factual and fair. A loaded comparison in a small trade where everyone talks will cost you more trust than it wins.
- **Don't fabricate proof.** Every peer win must be real and opted-in. A fake testimonial in this community travels faster than a real one and ends you.

- Exact competitor name for any comparison page.
